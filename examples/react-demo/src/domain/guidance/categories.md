# Project Categories

Every application picks one project category. This page defines what
each category covers, since some projects could plausibly fit more
than one and applicants should pick the closest match.

## Youth Programs

Programs primarily serving people under 18: after-school programs,
mentorship, youth sports, tutoring, and similar. A program that serves
a mix of ages but is primarily structured around youth participation
still counts as Youth Programs.

## Environmental

Projects focused on the local environment: community gardens, cleanup
efforts, tree planting, and environmental education. A youth program
that happens to be about gardening should still be filed under Youth
Programs if its primary structure is a youth program, not Environmental.

## Arts and Culture

Public art, cultural festivals, performance spaces, and similar
projects whose primary purpose is artistic or cultural, not funding a
youth program or a food program that happens to include an art
component.

## Food Security

Food pantries, community meal programs, and efforts to improve access
to food in underserved areas.

## Emergency Relief

Short-term response to a specific recent event, such as a fire, flood,
or other disaster affecting the service area. Emergency Relief
applications are reviewed on a rolling basis rather than waiting for
the normal funding cycle.
